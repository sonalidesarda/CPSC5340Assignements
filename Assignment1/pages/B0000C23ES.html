pws
3.0 out of 5 starsOK price for OK quality
March 31, 2019
Format: DVDVerified Purchase
Overall, I found this set of nine PD James titles solid mystery fare. Roy Marsden portrayed an iconic though not one-dimensional Adam Dalgliesh. James excels in creating believable sympathetic characters which adds an interesting element of ambiguity. The murderer isn't necessarily evil or even deeply flawed which makes it difficult to guess who-done-it. The professional cast are often recognizable from other roles especially of BBC Dickens from the late 70s/early 80s. Storylines are generally another James strength as a writer. Though not having read the novels, I assume that the blame for the few bad or ridiculously incomplete plots falls on the scriptwriters. Unfortunately, production issues abound to an extent that drastically reduces the quality of the series. The first five movies mostly have soap opera lighting along with distracting exposure problems. The fifth, Devices and Desires, is even missing the final episode. Though from this point on the lighting is natural. I wouldn't advise spending more than $30 for this set because of the persistent production flaws.
Other issues mostly pertain to the ever present socialist indoctrination: Is this due to PD James or the BBC or both? A Taste for Death provides a particularly disturbing peek into the inner workings of the socialist soul. Plot spoiler alert: Newly promoted female associate endures demand to play traditional role as caregiver for the elderly grandmother who raised her. Grandma gets taken hostage and gets killed when police woman granddaughter wrestles for gun. End scene: Policewoman thanks Dalgliesh for promotion and confesses maybe she subconsciously directed gunfire to feeble grandma to free herself from the burden. Ugh! I would've loved for my grandparents to have lived to a grouchy, arthritic one hundred if they'd been pleased to do so. Was James or the screenwriter dropping acid with Betty Friedan before writing this: A taste for death, indeed!
Anyway, maybe buy this if you're a fan of James and aren't too shocked at the often bizarrely incoherent and inconsistent socialist dogma (being disabled is fine just not too decrepit?). Lots of old tv series on Amazon for a similar price. Just sayin'.
