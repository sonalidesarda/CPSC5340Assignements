Editorial Reviews

Along the southern Line Islands lay a series of coral reefs teeming with sharks and exotic ocean life. Join leading marine ecologists and National Geographic Emerging Explorer and Fellow, Enric Sala, as they explore this utopia for sharks and discover valuable clues about how to preserve this delicate ecosystem. In an expedition of a lifetime, National Geographic investigates the amazing web of life that exists on one of the planet's last, pristine coral reefs.

4.0 out of 5 starsGreat documentary but maybe not the best pick for kids
March 24, 2011
Format: DVDVerified Purchase
Our 2 year old son LOVES sharks, so I bought this hoping that he would like it. While he is interested in the shark and fish footage, there is not as much as I had hoped for. The documentary is about a team of scientists exploring several coral reefs to see how the shark population is affected by the health of the reefs. So there is a lot of dialogue and footage of the team gathering information and talking about what they are finding. I felt like the documentary was more about the coral reefs than about sharks.

However, Mom and Dad enjoyed the documentary - it is very interesting. And there is a bonus documentary about sting rays (still not geared toward kids). Maybe older children would enjoy this more, once they are able to understand the information being given. But for the little guys, they just want to see the fish and sharks so they don't really get into the information side of things. Since we plan to home school, we are going to keep this DVD around - maybe our son will like it more later!
